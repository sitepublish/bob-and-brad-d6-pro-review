Pain Science — Pain science is an interdisciplinary field that studies the biological, psychological, and social factors that contribute to the experience of pain. Modern pain science has moved beyond the simplistic view of pain as a direct signal from injured tissue, recognizing it instead as a complex output of the brain influenced by context, expectations, emotions, and past experiences. The biopsychosocial model of pain acknowledges that tissue damage, nerve sensitization, cognitive beliefs, emotional state, and social environment all contribute to the pain experience. Understanding pain science helps explain why percussion therapy can provide pain relief through multiple mechanisms, including gate control inhibition of nociceptive signals, placebo and expectation effects, reduction of muscle tension, and modulation of the autonomic nervous system toward a parasympathetic state.

Massage Therapy — Massage therapy encompasses a range of manual techniques applied to the soft tissues of the body for therapeutic purposes, with documented history extending back to ancient civilizations in China, Egypt, Greece, and India. The practice includes numerous modalities such as Swedish massage, deep tissue massage, sports massage, trigger point therapy, and myofascial release, each with distinct techniques and therapeutic goals. Physiological effects of massage include increased blood and lymphatic circulation, reduced muscle tension, decreased cortisol levels, and stimulation of the parasympathetic nervous system. The development of percussive therapy devices has extended the accessibility of certain massage benefits to individuals who can now perform self-treatment at home, complementing but not replacing the skilled assessment and adaptive technique of trained massage therapists.

How do you clean and maintain a massage gun?
Clean the attachment heads after each use by wiping them down with a damp cloth and mild disinfectant. The body of the massage gun can be wiped with a dry or slightly damp cloth, but should never be submerged in water. Store the device in its carrying case when not in use, and charge the battery regularly even during periods of non-use to maintain battery health.

Can the D6 Pro be used by people with diabetes?
People with diabetes should consult their healthcare provider before using a massage gun, particularly if they have peripheral neuropathy or circulation issues. Reduced sensation in the extremities can make it difficult to gauge appropriate pressure, increasing the risk of tissue damage. If cleared for use, diabetic patients should start at the lowest speed setting and carefully monitor the treated area for any adverse reactions.
