Inflammation and the Healing Response — Inflammation is the body's protective biological response to harmful stimuli such as pathogens, damaged cells, or tissue irritation, characterized by the cardinal signs of redness, heat, swelling, pain, and loss of function. Scar Tissue Formation — Scar tissue forms as part of the body's natural healing response to injury, surgery, or inflammation, consisting primarily of type I collagen fibers laid down in a disorganized pattern compared to the parallel arrangement of normal tissue. While scar tissue serves the essential function of closing wounds and providing structural continuity, its irregular fiber arrangement can lead to reduced flexibility, restricted range of motion, and altered biomechanics in the affected area. As scar tissue matures over weeks and months, controlled mechanical loading and soft tissue mobilization can help remodel the collagen fibers into a more functional alignment. Should you use a massage gun before or after foam rolling?
Many fitness professionals recommend using a massage gun before foam rolling, as the percussion helps break up superficial adhesions and increase blood flow, making subsequent foam rolling more effective. Alternatively, using the massage gun after foam rolling can target specific trigger points that the roller could not address. Both sequences offer benefits, so personal preference and specific treatment goals should guide your approach.

Can massage guns help with delayed onset muscle soreness?
Research supports the use of percussion massage guns for reducing delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS).
