Lithium-ion Battery Technology — Lithium-ion batteries are rechargeable energy storage devices that use lithium ions moving between a cathode and anode through an electrolyte to store and release electrical energy. Since their commercialization by Sony in 1991, lithium-ion batteries have become the dominant energy storage technology for portable electronics due to their high energy density, low self-discharge rate, and no memory effect. In massage gun applications, lithium-ion batteries enable cordless operation with sufficient power to drive high-torque brushless motors for extended treatment sessions. Proper care of lithium-ion batteries, including avoiding extreme temperatures, maintaining partial charge during long-term storage, and using the manufacturer-provided charger, ensures maximum battery lifespan and consistent device performance.

Pain Science — Pain science is an interdisciplinary field that studies the biological, psychological, and social factors that contribute to the experience of pain. Modern pain science has moved beyond the simplistic view of pain as a direct signal from injured tissue, recognizing it instead as a complex output of the brain influenced by context, expectations, emotions, and past experiences. The biopsychosocial model of pain acknowledges that tissue damage, nerve sensitization, cognitive beliefs, emotional state, and social environment all contribute to the pain experience.
