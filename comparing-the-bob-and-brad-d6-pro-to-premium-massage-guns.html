Muscle Spasm — A muscle spasm is a sudden, involuntary contraction of one or more muscles that can cause significant pain and temporary loss of function. Spasms can result from muscle fatigue, dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, nerve irritation, or direct trauma to the muscle tissue. The mechanism involves an abnormal excitation of the motor nerve or the muscle fiber itself, leading to sustained contraction without the normal relaxation phase. Percussion therapy can help resolve muscle spasms by increasing blood flow to the affected area, stimulating the Golgi tendon organ to trigger reflexive relaxation, and providing sensory input that interrupts the spasm cycle through the gate control mechanism.

Muscle Physiology — Skeletal muscle is a form of striated muscle tissue that is under voluntary control of the somatic nervous system and is responsible for producing movement, maintaining posture, and generating heat. Each muscle is composed of bundles of muscle fibers called fascicles, which are in turn made up of individual muscle cells called myofibers containing the contractile proteins actin and myosin. Muscle contraction occurs through the sliding filament mechanism, where cross-bridges form between actin and myosin filaments in response to calcium ion release triggered by a nerve impulse. Understanding muscle physiology is essential for effective percussion therapy application, as the depth, frequency, and duration of percussion must account for muscle fiber orientation, tissue density, and the physiological state of the muscle being treated.

Can a massage gun help with lower back pain?
Massage guns can be effective for relieving muscular lower back pain by targeting the erector spinae, quadratus lumborum, and gluteal muscles. However, it is important to avoid applying percussion directly to the spine or bony prominences. Users should focus on the soft tissue surrounding the lumbar region and consult a healthcare provider if pain persists or worsens.
