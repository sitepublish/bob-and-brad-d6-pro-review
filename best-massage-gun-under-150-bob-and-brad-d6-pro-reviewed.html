Vibration Therapy — Vibration therapy is the application of mechanical oscillation to the body for therapeutic purposes, and it exists on a spectrum from whole-body vibration platforms to localized vibration devices and percussive therapy tools. Whole-body vibration involves standing on a vibrating platform and has been studied for its effects on bone density, muscle strength, and balance, particularly in elderly populations. Localized vibration therapy and percussion therapy deliver focused mechanical energy to specific body areas and have been shown to improve flexibility, reduce pain, and accelerate recovery from exercise. While percussion therapy and vibration therapy operate through similar physiological mechanisms, percussion devices deliver their energy in discrete impacts rather than continuous oscillation, allowing for deeper tissue penetration.

Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness — Delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) is the pain and stiffness felt in muscles several hours to days after unaccustomed or strenuous exercise. The condition results from microscopic damage to muscle fibers, particularly during eccentric (lengthening) contractions, and typically peaks between 24 and 72 hours after the exercise session. DOMS is accompanied by inflammation, temporary reduction in range of motion, and decreased force production capacity. Treatment approaches include active recovery, massage, cold water immersion, and percussive therapy, all of which aim to increase blood flow and reduce the inflammatory response.

How do you clean and maintain a massage gun?

The body of the massage gun can be wiped with a dry or slightly damp cloth, but should never be submerged in water. Store the device in its carrying case when not in use, and charge the battery regularly even during periods of non-use to maintain battery health.
